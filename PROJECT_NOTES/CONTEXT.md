### English Translation

A piece of land overlooking the sea, which the family of grandfather Panagiotis Tswnis used for growing vineyards, gave the idea around 1975 for the creation of the camping "Loutsa".

This idea was realized in 1980 by Nikolaos Tswnis.

The few foreign visitors (mainly Germans, Austrians, Italians) who were looking for pristine unexplored places in those times, found refuge and camped wherever the road led them. Thus, gradually the idea was born, and the fence of the property fell, providing access to any wandering tourist and making them feel safe within private land.

At first with some basic facilities such as water from the well, a few fresh eggs and vegetables from the farm. And later with some covered sanitary spaces and a kitchen, tourists did not delay in showing their preference over time.

In 1990 the camping closes to reopen two years later as "Camping Loutsa" officially now with the urban planning layout that it has to this day and with all the facilities and licenses of an organized tourist business.

"Camping Loutsa" is owned by Nikolaos Tswnis, who together with his family provide service, courtesy and friendliness to all visitors who will choose the camping for their holidays.

## SECTION: Finikounda - The Village

### Overview

Finikounda is a charming tourist fishing village located in Messinia, part of the Pylos-Nestor municipality. It sits strategically positioned midway between Methoni and Koroni, 70 km from Kalamata. The village serves as an ideal base for exploring the wider Peloponnese region.

---

### History & Origins

#### Population
- **Permanent residents**: Approximately 700 inhabitants
- Small, intimate community with authentic Greek character

#### Name Origin
Named by the **Phoenicians** (ancient Foinikes) - a seafaring people who used Finikounda as a naval station for:
- Trade commerce
- Maritime operations
- Historical occupation (piracy era)

#### Historical Development
- **1850s (Mid-19th Century)**: Village established in its current form
- **Original Name**: "Taverna" - named after the single tavern that existed in the settlement at that time
- **1980s**: Tourism development begins
- **Present Day**: Fully developed modern tourist destination

---

### Modern Amenities & Services

#### Accommodation
- Modern, quality hotel units
- Organized camping facilities (including Loutsa Camping)
- Diverse lodging options for all budgets

#### Dining & Fresh Seafood
- Multiple fish restaurants (psarotavernes)
- Traditional Greek tavernas
- Waterfront dining along the beach
- **Specialty**: Fresh local fish & traditional Greek cuisine

#### Shopping & Entertainment
- Tourist shops & boutiques
- Cafés & coffee shops
- Bars for evening entertainment & nightlife
- Full range of visitor services

---
